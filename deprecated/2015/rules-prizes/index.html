Rules & Prizes

Hack Rice organizers reserve the right to modify the contest rules as they see necessary and make judgments on issues not explicitly address in this document.

Application & Attendance

Any US student over the age of 18 is eligible to apply to HackRice 2015. Upon acceptance, the student must arrange their own travel to the event. Rice alumni are eligible to attend the event to hack or mentor other students. However, alumni's submissions will not be eligible for prizes. Alumni are welcome to make submissions and present their hacks during first round of judging.

Hackathon

HackRice will officially kick off at 11:30 AM on Saturday, January 31, 2015. We will provide tables, power strips, food, caffeine, and hourly raffle prizes to keep you awake. To be eligible for prizes, your submission must:

  • be created by team members entirely between the Saturday kick off and Sunday deadline
  • be created by a team with at most 5 members
  • External Resources: Participants may use external resources (online resources, reference books, help from mentors, etc), but the material of the submission must be created by team members alone.
  • Third Party Libraries and APIs: Third party libraries and APIs are permitted if they publicly available and free to use.

Submission

Each team is allowed to make a single submission by the deadline. The submission process will be done via ChallengePost and explained during the event. In general, the following items will be expected for the submission:

  • Team members, including contact information.
  • Application name and its description and purpose.
  • A 2 minutes max video demonstrating the hack.
  • URL to live demo of the application if applicable.

Demos and Judging

Following submission, everyone will present their hacks to the judges "science fair style". This means that the judges will walk around and ask you to present your project. The judges will select about 10 of projects to move onto the finals.

The finalists will have an opportunity to present their applications on Sunday evening to the audience and the panel of judges.

We will not judge code quality or style. However, it is recommended that you write clear and readable code to enable your team to work more efficiently.

Prizes

The following prizes will be awarded and split among the winning teams by judging the following criteria: innovation & creativity, technical complexity, and polish. Also, a single team cannot be awarded more than one of these prizes.

  • 1st Place: $3000
  • 2nd Place: $1500
  • 3rd Place: $1000
  • Best Use of Hardware Award: $1000
  • People's Choice Award: $500

Additional Prizes:

  • A Prize Raffled Every Hour
  • Two Sigma Best Data Visualization/Analytics Award
  • Indeed Most Helpful App Award
  • Microsoft best use of Microsoft product Award
  • Best use of Ebay/PayPal/Venmo API
